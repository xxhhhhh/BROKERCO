Review of Moon.Market

Moon.Market is a platform for quick selling of in-game items from CS2, Rust, Dota 2, and Team Fortress 2. It offers competitive prices and a variety of withdrawal options, including cryptocurrencies, bank cards, and e-wallets.

The website features a clean and intuitive interface, and transactions are processed swiftly. This makes it convenient for users looking to quickly convert their in-game items into real-world value.

Overall, Moon.Market is a reliable and efficient choice for instant item sales. Its user-friendly design and flexible payout options make it appealing to a wide range of users.

Trust

Moon.Market has been operating since 2019, offering fast sale services for skins from popular games. Despite stable operations and high user activity, the platform maintains a moderate trust level due to limited transparency—such as undisclosed ownership and absence from major independent ratings. However, thousands of positive user reviews and long-term market presence confirm that the platform is legitimate and generally safe with reasonable caution.

Support

Moon.Market’s support service operates 24/7 and is accessible via Telegram and the website FAQ. Users report fast responses and quick resolution of most issues, especially when online support is available. However, there may be cases of incomplete or delayed assistance, particularly during technical disruptions. Overall, the support system earns a positive rating, especially for response speed.

Payments

The platform supports various payout methods: bank cards, e-wallets, SBP, and cryptocurrencies. Funds are usually received within 10–30 minutes, making the process highly convenient. A key point is the minimum withdrawal amount of 500 RUB, which may be limiting for some users. Payout processing also depends on support’s online status, which should be considered when planning transactions. Otherwise, the financial system is user-friendly with no hidden fees.

Pricing

Moon.Market offers fixed prices for skins based on market demand and item quality. The price level is typical for instant-buy platforms—around 50% of Steam value. Although competitive, users note occasional price fluctuations and manipulation, making regular selling less profitable. Still, for quick one-time deals, the terms remain attractive.
