Review of LOOT.Farm

LOOT.Farm is a trading platform that enables users to exchange in-game items from CS2, Rust, Dota 2, and Team Fortress 2. With a broad selection of skins at various price points, it caters to both casual and experienced traders.

A key strength of LOOT.Farm is its transparency — every Steam bot holding tradeable items is clearly visible, building user trust. The platform also offers a unique auction system for CS2 items, adding an interactive element to trading.

According to public data, LOOT.Farm handles over 50,000 trades per day and sees more than 500,000 monthly visitors. Deposits can be made via various third-party services with a wide range of supported payment methods.

Overall, LOOT.Farm stands out as a reliable and legitimate platform for safe and efficient in-game item trading, supported by high traffic, transparency, and item variety.

Trust

LOOT.Farm has been operating since 2017 and is considered one of the most stable platforms for automatic in-game item trading. The platform ensures full transparency: each bot is publicly displayed, allowing users to track item locations precisely. High user activity, the absence of major scandals, and a well-designed trade system reinforce trust among traders and gamers.

Support

LOOT.Farm provides support via contact form and email. Users report that responses can be delayed, especially during peak hours. However, the knowledge base and in-platform hints help solve most issues without needing to contact support. A premium subscription offers priority support, which can be valuable for active traders.

Payments

The financial system on LOOT.Farm is limited: deposits are available through third-party payment gateways, but fiat or crypto withdrawals are not supported. All funds remain within the platform and can only be used for item purchases. This makes the service convenient for trading, but not suitable for those seeking real-money withdrawals.

Pricing

The main drawback of LOOT.Farm is its uncompetitive pricing. While the commission is a moderate 3–5%, the internal item valuation system often underprices skins, and purchases may be significantly more expensive than the market rate. Even with cashback and bonuses, the platform falls short in price appeal compared to other trading sites.
